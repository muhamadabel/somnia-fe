# 16 — Glossary

# Purpose

This glossary defines important business terms used throughout Dream Journal Analyzer.

---

## Dream

A journal entry describing a user's dream.

---

## Dream Analysis

AI-generated interpretation and reflection based on a dream.

---

## Reflection

A supportive insight intended to encourage self-awareness.

Reflection is not a medical diagnosis.

---

## Emotion

A feeling detected from dream content.

Examples:

- Joy
- Fear
- Sadness
- Curiosity
- Calm

---

## Dream Symbol

A recurring object, place, person, or event identified within dreams.

Examples:

- Water
- Forest
- Flying
- Falling
- Fire

---

## Visualization

An AI-generated image representing the dream.

---

## Dream History

The complete collection of dreams recorded by a user.

---

## Emotion Trend

Changes in emotional patterns over time.

---

## Wellness Report

A periodic summary generated from historical dream data.

---

## AI Companion

A conversational assistant capable of answering questions using the user's dream history.

---

## Community Post

A dream voluntarily shared with other users.

Private dreams remain private unless explicitly shared.

---

## Context

Relevant historical information retrieved before AI generates a response.

Context may include:

- Previous Dreams
- Previous Analyses
- Emotion Trends
- Conversation History

---

## Memory

Persistent information used by the AI Companion to provide contextual responses over time.

---

## Reflection Question

A question generated by AI to encourage deeper personal reflection rather than provide definitive answers.

---

## Dashboard

The main overview screen displaying dream activity, emotional insights, and quick actions.

---

## Report Period

The timeframe used to generate a wellness report, such as weekly, monthly, or yearly.

---

## Soft Delete

A deletion strategy where records are hidden from normal use but remain recoverable for a period of time.

---

## Audit Log

A record of important system events used for security monitoring and troubleshooting.

---

## Provider

An external service used by the application, such as AI models, storage, authentication, or notifications.

The implementation should allow providers to be replaced without significant architectural changes.
